Hungarian yellow bean soup

In Hungary the most widely planted bean type is yellow bean, this variety is more popular than green bean.

Yellow beans belong to the family of snap beans, which can be eaten pod and all.

Ingridients:

  • 800 g (~1 3/4 lb) yellow beans
  • 1-2 dsp (2-4 tsp) salt
  • 2 l (8 cups) water
  • 3 garlic cloves (or 1 medium onion)
  • 2 tbsp lard
  • 2 heaping tbsp flour
  • 1 dsp (2 tsp) ground paprika
  • 2 heaping tbsp sour cream
  • 4-5 sprigs of dill (or parsley)
  • sugar and vinegar to taste

Steps:

  1. Trim the yellow beans top and tail and cut into pieces.
  2. In a pot of salted water cook the beans until tender.
  3. In the meantime make the roux.
  4. Heat up the lard, add finely chopped garlic (or finely chopped onion) and gently cook until translucent.
  5. Sprinkle with two tablespoons of flour and stir for a few seconds till the roux reaches the blond stage.
  6. Turn off the heat.
  7. Add paprika and combine.
  8. Let the roux cool.
  9. When the beans are soft, ladle some liquid to the roux to dilute.
  10. Stir until smooth and lump-free.
  11. Pour the mixture in a fine stream to the beans while stirring continuously.
  12. Cook the soup for 8-10 minutes.
  13. Check if it's salty enough, add more salt if needed.
  14. Place sour cream in a small bowl.
  15. When the soup is ready, turn off the heat, add a ladle of hot soup to the sour cream and whisk to combine.
  16. Gradually pour the diluted sour cream into the soup while stirring, and finally add finely chopped dill (or parsley).
  17. After serving it can be flavoured with sugar and/or vinegar
