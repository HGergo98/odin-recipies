Goulash soup

The name originates from the Hungarian gulyás, which means herdsman. But over time the word has got a second meaning, a meat dish that was prepared by herdsmen.

Ingridients:

  • 800 g (~1 3/4 lb) beef, cubed
  • 500 g (~1 lb) potatoes, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 parsley roots, diced
  • 1 big onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp lard
  • 2 tbsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp hot paprika
  • 1 green pepper, diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • 3-4 tsp salt
  • 1,5-2 l (~8-9 cups) water
  • 100 g (~3/4 cup) all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1-2 tsp water

Steps:

  1. In a heavy pot heat the lard.
  2. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add garlic and beef, and braise until all sides turn white.
  4. Remove from the heat, sprinkle paprika over the meat, stir to cover evenly, pour in water and return to the heat.
  5. Add caraway seeds, green pepper, bay leaf, salt and pepper, bring it to a boil, cover and cook over low heat.
  6. In the meantime make the dough for the noodles.
  7. Place flour and egg in a bowl, and knead them together into a smooth dough.
  8. The dough should be shapeable, but not too soft.
  9. If you find the dough too tough, add some water.
  10. Pinch fingernail-sized bits from the dough and place them on a kitchen towel.
  11. Once the meat is nearly done, add diced carrots and parsley roots to the pot.
  12. When carrots are half cooked, add diced potatoes and pinched noodles (both of them need 20-25 minutes to be tender).
  13. Serve hot with white bread.
